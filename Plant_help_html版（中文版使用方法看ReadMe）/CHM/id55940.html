Priority
SimTalk Reference > General SimTalk Functions > Control Flow Statements > Suspending Methods > The Waituntil-Statement and the Stopuntil-Statement > Priority

Plant Simulation may suspend several methods based on identical or similar conditions. Consequently, a set of several Methods may be reactivated at the same time. Then, the interpreter reactivates the Method with the highest priority.

After it finished executing this Method or it comes across another waituntil-statement with an unfulfilled condition during its execution, the interpreter re-analyzes the conditions of the remaining Methods in the set. This yields a new subset of Methods to be activated. If any of the conditions are still true, the interpreter re-analyzes the priorities and again selects the Method with the highest priority.

For evaluating the priority you can use methods, tables, or parameters. Avoid "side effects," such as deleting MUs or changing a global variable (Variable), as the frequency and time of evaluation of the priority expression depend on both the interpreter and the state of the model.

One (1) is the lowest priority. The higher the values you enter, the higher the priority, i.e., the sooner the Method will be reactivated.
