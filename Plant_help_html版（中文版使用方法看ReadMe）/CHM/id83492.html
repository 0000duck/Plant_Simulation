Model an Accumulating/a Non-Accumulating Conveyor Between Stations
Step-by-Step Help > Modeling in 2D > Modeling Transport Systems > Modeling a Transport System with Active Objects of Type Conveyor > Model an Accumulating/a Non-Accumulating Conveyor Between Stations

You can also use the Conveyor to model an accumulating or a non-accumulating transport system between stations.

  • To make the MUs accumulate on the Conveyor, select the check box Accumulating. This allows the MUs to move front to end to each other, when the exit of the conveyor is blocked.

  • To make the MUs retain their distance to each other, i.e., make all succeeding MUs stop moving when the preceding MU cannot exit, clear the check box to deactivate Accumulating.

As we want to apply the default behavior of the Conveyor, we do not change its Capacity of -1, which stands for an infinite number of MUs. This way its capacity is defined by its own length and by the length of the parts (MU), which it transports.

In our sample we entered an Availability of 85 percent and an MTTR of 360 seconds for the two Stations, SP1 and SP2.

Note that Plant Simulation marks a failed failure profile red.

You can use an accumulating Conveyor to decouple stations. Then, the Conveyor behaves like a roller conveyor that allows the MUs to move up to those in front of them, when the exit of the conveyor is blocked.

When you do not decouple the stations, the Conveyor behaves like a belt conveyor, meaning that the belt stops when an MU blocks the exit of the Conveyor and restarts as soon as the MU at the exit has moved on to the successor.
