Model a Simple Conveyor Between Two Stations
Step-by-Step Help > Modeling in 2D > Modeling Transport Systems > Modeling a Transport System with Active Objects of Type Conveyor > Model a Simple Conveyor Between Two Stations

In its most basic way, a transport system consists of a conveyor that moves parts between two processing stations. In our example we inserted a Conveyor between two Stations, SP1 and SP2. The MUs are transferred from the point-oriented station SP1 into the length-oriented Conveyor, which then transports them to the point-oriented station SP2.

The Conveyor moves the MUs from the station SP1 with the Final speed we enter to the station SP2. Plant Simulation computes the transport Time using the final speed and the Length of the Conveyor. Note that speed, length and transport time depend on each other. When you change speed or length, Plant Simulation recalculates the transport time anew. Changing the transport time also changes the speed.
