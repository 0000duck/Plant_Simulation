Relative Occupation
2D Reference Help > Material Flow Objects > Shared Properties of the Material Flow Objects > Statistics of the Material Flow Objects > Resource Statistics > Relative Occupation

The relative occupancy is determined by the sum of all dwelling times of all MUs during the statistics collection period during which the object was not Paused and not Failed, divided by the capacity and the entire statistics collection period without counting pauses and failures. The return value 0 stands for 0 percent, the return value 1 stands for 100 percent.

Take a ParallelStation for example with four processing stations that processes a single MU during the entire statistics collection period. It has a relative occupancy of 25 percent.

You can also use this method to compute the average waiting time of an MU: 

EventController.SimTime * Buffer.StatRelativeOccupation * Buffer.Capacity / Buffer.StatNumIn

Relative Occupation with Interruptions

The relative occupancy with interruptions is the sum of all dwelling times of all MUs on the object during the statistics collection period, divided by the capacity and the entire statistics collection period.
