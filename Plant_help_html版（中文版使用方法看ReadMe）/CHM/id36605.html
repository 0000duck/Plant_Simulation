Reactivating Methods
SimTalk Reference > General SimTalk Functions > Control Flow Statements > Suspending Methods > The Waituntil-Statement and the Stopuntil-Statement > Reactivating Methods > Reactivating Methods

The value of a condition leading to a method being suspended can change because of a simulation event (for example a leaving MU) or method processing (for example, assignment of a value). When a method is active, the interpreter interrupts the execution of the active method and saves the method’s entire call chain.

After that, the interpreter re-analyzes the conditions of the suspended methods. The interpreter does not analyze conditions that do not depend on the change again. If the condition is not true, it suspends the methods immediately. If the condition is true, the interpreter analyzes its priority and builds a priority list. It then selects the method with the highest priority and resumes executing that method. The method execution continues until Plant Simulation executed the entire present call chain or the method is suspended again.
